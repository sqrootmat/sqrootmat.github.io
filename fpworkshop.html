Future Planning Workshop Design & Synthesis

Mitra Gusheh, Tyler Key, Varun Mathew & Dianne Moy

Centre for Social Justice and Inclusion, 2019

Strengths Utilised

Synthesis of Qualitative Insights
Uncovering the changes that soft systems required for a positive future shift of volunteer experiences.
Synthesis of Technical Requirements
Extracting and communicating the hard system shifts required for the future systems to be able to manage a scaling operation.
Workshop Facilitation
I needed to demonstrate effective communication whilst being able to distil and convey complex topics.

I was involved in the co-design and co-facilitation of a future planning workshop run by the Centre for Social Justice and Inclusion and the Faculty of Transdisciplinary Innovation for Primary Ethics, a volunteer organisation. This workshop was primarily focussed on understanding the direction that Primary Ethics was heading and the changes required in order for the organisation to scale and realise their goals.

Prior to the workshop, I was in charge of creating a volunteer journey map that the workshop was centred around - to assist in envisioning the current and future states. Following the workshop, I took on the role of a research assistant where my goal was to take the insights from the workshop and develop documentation of the technical requirements and a synthesis of the qualitative insights to contribute to the ideation and design of better volunteer experiences. This highlighted the importance of understanding the significance of having a shift in both hard and soft systems in order to create a holistic and beneficial change.
